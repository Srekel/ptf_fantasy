Rules

Action Tests

  1. Rolls on actions performed by character are rolled with a number of D6 (six-sided dice).
  2. Before rolling, the character may Concentrate: Discard two or more D6 and replace them with one D8 for every pair of D6.
  3. After rolling, the player can use one or more Fate Points to affect the result. Each FP can:
    • Reroll one die.
    • Increase the value of one die AND reduce another die.
    • Add one more die to the roll.
  4. The result of a D6 die roll counts as following:
    • 1: Critical Fail (CF)
    • 2-3: Fail
    • 4-5: Success
    • 6: Critical Success (CS)
    In other words, equal chance of succeding or failing.
  5. The result of a D8 die roll counts as following:
    • 1-2: Fail
    • 3-8: Success
    In other words, 25% chance of Fail, 75% chance of Success.
  6. Each CS cancels out a CF (discard both form result). If there are no CFs, it cancels a Fail. Similarly, CFs cancels CSs and Successes.
  7. When done Fate Pointing, the follow effect happens:
    • 1 CF:
    • 1 CF:
    • 1 CS:
    • 1 CF:
  8. If there are more ones than total successes, the action has critically failed, and vice versa for critical successes.
  9. 1D6 means: One six sided die.
  10. AD6 means: Look at character's Agility. Roll that many D6.
  11. SD6, WD6, CD6 means: Same as AD6 but look at Strength, Wisdom, Charisma.
  12. AD6 * Strength for an action means: Roll AD6. Count the number of successes. Multiply that number with the character's Strength.
